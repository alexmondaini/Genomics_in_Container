When looking for primer generation I took into account several general rules, this being:

1. GC-content(40%-60%) / GC positively correlates with melting temperature (Tm)
2. primer length (16bp-30bp)
3. melting temperatur (Tm 58 - 60 degrees celsius)
4. difference in Tm among forward and revers primers (3 degrees celsius maximum)
5. avoid non-specific binding to other organisms of interest (homo sapiens,influenza A, RSV)
6. self-complementarity (lower value is better)
7. 3' and 5' end having G/C content is better
8. Forward primers generally are less sensitive to base mismatching or SNPs, 3' ends should not have more than 3 base mismatches, if the base mismatch of 3' is at the very end avoid it. Better design a few reverse primers just in case.
